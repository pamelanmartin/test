About This Tutorial

When you search for information, you'll encounter many different types of publications. Knowing the difference helps you find the right source for your purpose.

This tutorial will help you distinguish between three major types of articles:

Popular Magazines
Written for a general audience on everyday topics
Scholarly Journals
Research written by and for experts and academics
Trade Publications
Industry news written for professionals in a field
Popular Magazines

What Are Popular Magazine Articles?

Popular magazines are typically focused on current events or general interests. Their purpose is to inform, entertain, or elicit an emotional response from readers.

They are often written by staff writers, journalists, or freelancers, and are intended for the general public. Because of this, the language avoids heavy technical jargon and terminology.

Popular magazine articles generally do not cite their sources. However, some respected magazines publish substantial, well-researched content that can be useful as a starting point for research.

Key Characteristics

Focused on current events or general interest topics
Written by journalists, staff writers, or freelancers
Intended for the general public
Informal, accessible language — no technical jargon
Sources are usually not cited
Typically includes photos, advertisements, and visual content
Scholarly Journals

What Are Scholarly Journal Articles?

Scholarly journals typically contain research results, literature reviews, and book reviews. Their purpose is to share new research or scholarship with the academic community.

The writing style is very formal and technical, using discipline-specific vocabulary. Articles are written by professors, scholars, or researchers, and are intended for other scholars, researchers, and students.

Scholarly journal articles go through a process called peer review — other experts in the same field (called peers or referees) evaluate the work before it is published. A "peer-reviewed" label is a strong indicator of quality and credibility.

Authors always cite their sources in reference lists or footnotes.

Key Characteristics

Contains research results, reviews, and academic scholarship
Written by professors, scholars, or researchers
Intended for scholars, researchers, and students
Formal, technical language with discipline-specific terms
Always includes citations / reference lists
Goes through peer review before publication
Trade Publications

What Are Trade Publications?

Trade publications contain articles about a specific profession, business, or industry. Their purpose is to inform about business or industry news, trends, and products.

They are written by staff writers, business professionals, and industry professionals. The audience is similarly specialized — business and industry professionals in that particular field.

Trade publications look similar to popular magazines — they often have lots of advertisements — but the content is focused toward a narrow, professional audience rather than the general public.

Key Characteristics

Focused on a specific profession, industry, or business sector
Written by staff writers and industry professionals
Intended for professionals in that industry
Less technical than scholarly; more specialized than popular
May or may not cite sources
Visually resembles popular magazines; often contains many ads
